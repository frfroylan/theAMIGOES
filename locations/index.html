Hike - Inspiration Point

    Trail is part of the dozen trails that lead up to Santa Ynez Mountains. From the top, the California cost is visible for hundredths of miles allowing you to see many of the Channel Islands proximal to Santa Barabra. The hike is not very long, therefore it’s recommended to engage in a bike trip from downtown Santa Barabra and then hike up the trail. Biking from Downtown Santa Barbara to Beginning of inspiration point is roughly an hour of time (5miles). The Hike begins on a paved service road and crosses a bridge over Mission Creek. The paved road ends where it turns into a dirt road. There are many swimming holes and several small and large waterfalls. Exploring the area off the main trail is possible, however caution must be taken due to the presence of poison oaks that grow around the creek.

How to get there:

    From US highway 101 in Santa Barbara take Mission Street off ramp and turn right at the light. Continue on Mission Street and turn right onto foothill road and then left onto Tunnel Road. You will arrive at a fork and take the left fork and ride all the way to the end. Lock up your bike where you see fit, parking is available on the right hand side of the road within the white lines.

What to expect:

King snakes, rattle snakes, large animals such as bears, bobcats, dear, coyotes, and even mountain lions

Hike - Seven Falls Hike

    A more intense hike than Inspiration Point but most people in shape can handle this hike, it must be stated that the trail involves rock hopping and climbing up rocky walls (no gear is required, just a good pair of shoes and knowing your limits). The hike is about 2.5 miles round trip with an elevation gain of 800ft. Activities include: Boulder hopping, swimming, waterfall jumping during wet season. The hike is slightly more north from inspiration point, (0.8miles i.e. ~30 minute walk).
